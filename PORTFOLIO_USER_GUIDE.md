## Key Interactive Features

### Clickable Dates — Gold Underlined Text
Throughout the report in Tabs 1, 2, and 3, dates appear highlighted in gold with a dashed underline. Click or tap any gold date to open a forensic analysis panel showing:
- The event that occurred on that date
- The forensic narrative explaining its legal significance
- The statutory framework breached
- The specific evidence files from that exact date with their SHA-256 integrity hashes
- Direct links to open and view those documents

**Example:** Click "16 Apr 2026" to see the High Court gag order documents, the forensic narrative of the concealment, and the full SHA-256 hash proving the document has not been altered.

### Clickable Reference Badges — Blue Boxes
Throughout the report, evidence reference codes appear as small blue boxes — B1, C1, E1, O1, M1, M2 etc. Click or tap any blue badge to open the evidence viewer for that specific document showing:
- The document description
- The full 64-character SHA-256 hexadecimal integrity hash
- A Copy Hash button
- The document itself — PDFs render inline, other formats open directly

**Example:** Click "O1" to open the CCMA opposing affidavit containing the false job title and the dismissal of clinical depression as a "cynical excuse."

### Evidence Vault Search — Tab 4
In the Dashboard tab, a search bar allows full-text search across all 881 sealed files. Type any word, name, institution, or case number to instantly filter the vault.

**Examples of searchable terms:**
- Person names: jason whyte, gerald van wyk, monwabisi kula, ntabiseng ngwane
- Institutions: ccma, sahrc, sanc, afrocentric, medscheme
- Legal terms: perjury, contempt, interdict, constructive dismissal
- Statutory references: section 162, pda, bcea, nhi
- Case numbers: wect23564-25, 2026-086906, 2026-077546

### Keyword Dropdown — Tab 4
Click the **🔑 Keywords** button in the Dashboard to open a dropdown of 80+ pre-categorised forensic keywords organised into seven categories:
- Legal Proceedings
- People
- Institutions
- Statutory References
- Forensic Governance
- Outcomes
- Roles

Click any keyword to instantly filter the vault to all documents containing that term.

### Browse by Date — Tab 4
Below the search bar in the Dashboard, gold date pills show all 29 dates for which evidence files are indexed. Click any date pill to see only the documents from that specific date — each displayed with its SHA-256 hash and a direct View Evidence button.

### SHA-256 Hash Verifier — Tab 4
At the top of the Dashboard, a hash verification tool allows independent verification of any document. Paste a 64-character SHA-256 hash and click Verify Hash. The system will either:
- Confirm the hash matches a sealed file and display the document
- Return a NOT FOUND result — indicating the document may have been tampered with or is not part of this submission

This tool gives Parliament, the SIU, or any oversight body the ability to independently verify the integrity of any document at any time.

## Understanding SHA-256 Cryptographic Integrity

Every document in this portfolio has been sealed with a SHA-256 cryptographic hash. This is a 64-character hexadecimal string — a mathematical fingerprint of the document's exact contents.

**What this means:**
- If even a single character in a document is changed after sealing, the hash changes completely
- The hash displayed in the viewer is the hash generated at the time of submission
- Any independent party can re-hash the document and compare — if the hashes match, the document is authentic and unaltered
- This is the same standard used by courts, law enforcement agencies, and international forensic bodies

**Example hash:**
`5f3d521b6ae610ea70baa25bb012cdee8809dcefaa2b84558578 2330cc655504`

This 64-character string is mathematically unique to one specific document. It cannot be forged or replicated for a different document.
