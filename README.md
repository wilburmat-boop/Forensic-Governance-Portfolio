# Forensic-Governance-Portfolio
Description for Parliamentary Review ​This dossier constitutes a forensic-grade evidence bundle compiled to document a sustained pattern of institutional non-compliance, procedural obstruction, and the resultant humanitarian crisis stemming from a protected disclosure.  
​The bundle is structured to provide Parliament with a chronological, verifiable account of how multiple state and regulatory safety nets failed to protect a whistleblower, directly contributing to professional erasure and imminent destitution.
​Core Components of the Dossier:
​Forensic Payroll Anomaly: Documentation of a material discrepancy between official payslip gross remuneration and statutory declarations made to government authorities (SARS/UIF), identifying potential statutory misrepresentation.
​Institutional Failure Matrix: A granular audit of engagement with nine regulatory bodies (including CCMA, DEL, SAPS, and Labour Court), demonstrating a pattern of institutional bias, cover-ups, and denial of the constitutional right of access to justice (Section 34).
​Verified Integrity Log: Every document contained within this bundle is subject to a SHA-256 hash-audit, ensuring the non-repudiation and integrity of the evidence provided for official parliamentary review.  
​Humanitarian Impact Assessment: A factual account of the causal chain between the respondent’s unlawful conduct—specifically the unauthorized 40.47% salary deduction—and the applicant's current status, characterized by the forced withdrawal of retirement savings, the seizure of personal property, and confirmed imminent homelessness.
​Regulatory Reference Chain: The dossier cross-references verbatim all primary correspondence with the South African Human Rights Commission (SAHRC) and the Council for Medical Schemes (CMS), establishing that the matters raised were consistently acknowledged for their "seriousness" yet met with systemic deflection.
​Purpose of Submission
​This evidence is submitted for independent investigation by the relevant Parliamentary Portfolio Committee. The objective is to demonstrate that the applicant’s experience is not an isolated employment dispute, but rather a "connected forensic account" of the structural failures that occur when a corporate entity operates in defiance of the Protected Disclosures Act and Basic Conditions of Employment Act while being shielded by institutional dysfunction.
