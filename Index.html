The Wilbur Method™

Proprietary Forensic Governance Framework · EthicHawks 2026

It comprises four interlocking constructs that, together, constitute a complete forensic account of institutional governance failure and its human cost.
I
Governance Tax™
The quantifiable, documented cost imposed on a whistleblower as a direct result of institutional non-compliance. Not a metaphor — a calculable financial and humanitarian impact that can be presented to a court or committee with precision.

Governance Paradox™
The structural condition in which oversight institutions fail precisely at the moment they are constitutionally mandated to function. The paradox is that the more serious the complaint, the more likely the institution is to deflect rather than act.
CCMA commissioner irregularities · Labour Court portal obstruction · Rule 42 procedural violations · Scale C costs against unrepresented whistleblower
III
Intellectual Extraction™
The systematic appropriation of a whistleblower's governance knowledge, clinical expertise, and intellectual property without attribution, compensation, or acknowledgement — often continuing after dismissal.

IV
Institutional Silence™
The coordinated pattern across multiple oversight bodies of formally acknowledging the seriousness of a complaint while taking no substantive protective action. Silence is not neutrality — it is the mechanism by which institutional complicity operates.
